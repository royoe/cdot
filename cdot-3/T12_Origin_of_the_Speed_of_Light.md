# T12 — The Origin of the Speed of Light

## Physical Concept

In standard physics, the speed of light $c$ is a fundamental constant — a fixed
parameter of the laws of nature with no explanation for why it takes the value it does.
This model proposes that $c$ is not a constant but a **dynamical, relational quantity**:
its value is set by the matter content of the universe.

This is the model's deepest organizing principle. Understanding it means understanding
why the model is genuinely Machian in character, what "Machian" means here, and what
remains open.

---

## The Relational Principle

The foundational statement is:

> **The global matter content of the universe sets the speed of light $c$;
> and $c$ sets every energy, length, time, and coupling. Therefore all physical
> scales are referred to the cosmic matter content.**

This is **counting-Machianism** — the model is Machian about $c$, not about inertial
mass. This distinguishes it from:

- **Sciama's Machianism** (potential-Machianism): inertial mass is generated by the
  gravitational potential of all other matter ($GM_u/(R_u c^2) \approx 1$). The model
  explicitly rejects this: the Sciama relation drifts as $c^{-10/3}$ and is incompatible
  with invariant mass (T11).
- **Brans-Dicke theory**: the gravitational coupling $G$ is a field sourced by
  matter. Related but not the same.
- **Standard VSL theories** (Barrow-Magueijo): $c$ varies but is not tied to a specific
  physical mechanism.

In this model, the counting principle $c \propto N$ is the mechanism: the speed of
light equals (proportionally) the number of particles within the causal horizon.

---

## Premise 2: Horizon Counting

The causal horizon grows at rate $dR/dt = c(t)$ into a static, uniform particle sea
of number density $n$. The number of particles within the horizon is:
$$N = n \cdot \tfrac{4}{3}\pi R^3 \propto R^3.$$
Premise 2 states $c \propto N \propto R^3$, the volume law. Substituting into
$dR/dt = c = kR^3$ gives the horizon ODE (Core Principles §3).

The relational content: in a universe with only $N$ particles, $c$ would be proportionally
smaller. In a universe with no particles, $c = 0$ — all kinematics become trivial (no
propagation, no clocks). Matter does not merely *exist in* a universe with a given $c$;
the matter *creates* the kinematic arena.

---

## The Premise Fork: Particle Count vs. Classical Mach

Premise 2 has two natural formulations that coincide for invariant mass in the current
epoch but diverge at the temporal extremes.

### Particle count ($c \propto N$)

$c$ is proportional to the number of particles — massive or not — in the causal horizon.
This is the count of quanta, regardless of their rest mass.

### Classical Mach ($c \propto M_u$)

$c$ is proportional to the total rest mass of the universe (within the horizon).
This is closer to the original Machian spirit (Mach 1883; Sciama 1953) and to
Dirac's large-number hypothesis.

### Why they agree in the present epoch

With invariant rest mass $m$ per baryon, $M_u = N_\text{baryons} \cdot m$, so
$c \propto N_\text{baryons}$ and $c \propto M_u$ are equivalent as long as
$\langle m \rangle = M_u/N$ is constant. For a baryon-dominated universe at the
present epoch, this holds.

### Where they diverge

**Early universe — radiation-dominated era.** The universe was once dominated by
relativistic species: photons, neutrinos, and electron-positron pairs. If "particles
that count" includes relativistic quanta, then $N$ includes $\sim 10^9$ photons and
neutrinos per baryon. Classical Mach ($c \propto M_u$) gives zero weight to massless
photons and nearly zero to relativistic neutrinos, while count ($c \propto N$) would
be dominated by them.

Whether "only real massive particles count" or "every quantum counts" is the
controlling question. It determines whether $c$ during the radiation era was dominated
by the few baryons or by the vast relativistic sea.

**Connecton/genesis bootstrap (T13, T14).** The genesis picture relies on a vast
number of primordial pair-creation events (momentary $e^+e^-$ pairs) to drive $c$
upward in the earliest moments. This requires the count reading: the transient pairs
boost $c$ by count, not by their near-zero rest mass. Under classical Mach, the
genesis bootstrap would be vastly weaker or absent entirely.

**Far future.** Under count (including the vast relic photon/neutrino sea), $N$ grows
as the horizon swallows more relic quanta → $c$ growth is open-ended, self-sustaining.
Under classical Mach, once baryogenesis and star formation cease, the massive content
is comoving-fixed → $c$ growth could stall once no new massive particles are created.
A profoundly different long-term cosmology.

### The neutrino-runaway check

If neutrinos count, could stellar production create so many neutrinos that $c$ grows
anomalously fast? Integrating stellar neutrino production over the entire stellar era
($\sim 10^{14}$ yr at today's rate) gives $\sim 10^{-5}$ of the relic neutrino sea.
The running total is dominated by primordial relic neutrinos; stellar production is
negligible. There is no neutrino runaway. So the count premise does not introduce a
stellar-burning instability.

### Current recommendation

Keep the fork open. Reverting to classical Mach would gain theoretical standardness
but would lose the two-ended self-sustaining engine (genesis bootstrap + open-ended
future). The count premise provides a mechanism (pair-creation boosts $c$ in the
primordial era); classical Mach does not.

**Observable discriminator:** Big Bang Nucleosynthesis (T13). The two premises predict
different $c$ during nucleosynthesis ($z \sim 10^{10}$), because they weight the same
relativistic species differently. Different $c$ gives different expansion rate and
freeze-out temperature, hence different light-element yields ($^4$He, D, $^7$Li).

## The Relational Principle as a Unifying Thread

Once one accepts $c \propto N$ and the scaling $\epsilon_0 \propto c^{-1}$ (forced by
EM), the relational principle flows through the entire structure:

| Domain | Consequence | Document |
|---|---|---|
| $c \propto N$ (root) | The kinematic arena is set by matter content | This document |
| Atomic frequencies $\nu \propto c^2$ | Time is referred to $N$ | T2, T1 |
| Redshift = fixed photon vs. $N$-changed reference | Observable manifestation | T2 |
| Orbital expansion $r \propto c^2$ | Lengths referred to $N$ | T9 |
| Galaxy sizes $r_e \propto (1+z)^{-1}$ | Structural sizes referred to $N$ | T10 |
| Energy non-conservation (relational reading) | Energies referred to $N$ | T11 |
| $G \propto c^{-2}$ (if derived) | Couplings referred to $N$ | T8 |
| Genesis bootstrap (T13) | Initial $N$ sets the entire cosmic history | T13 |

The relational principle is not a separate postulate — it is the $c \propto N$ premise
read as a global organizing principle. Everything that varies does so because $c$
varies; $c$ varies because the matter content ($N$) grows.

## The Sciama Relation: Why It Fails Here

Sciama's relation $GM_u/(R_u c^2) \approx 1$ looks like it could constrain $G$ via
the relational principle. In this model:
$$\frac{GM_u}{R_u c^2} = \frac{c^{-2} \cdot \tfrac{4}{3}\pi R^3 \rho}{R \cdot c^2} \propto \frac{R^2}{c^4}.$$
Using $c \propto R^3 \Rightarrow R \propto c^{1/3}$, so $R^2 \propto c^{2/3}$:
$$\frac{R^2}{c^4} \propto \frac{c^{2/3}}{c^4} = c^{2/3 - 4} = c^{-10/3}.$$
This ratio varies as $c^{-10/3}$
and is approximately $1/2$ today by coincidence, not by design. The model's Machianism
is in the counting rule ($c \propto N$), not in a gravitational-potential relation.
The two are genuinely different Machian hypotheses.

## Physical Content: What Does "$c \to 0$ for a Lone Particle" Mean?

If there is only one particle in the universe (or zero), then $N = 0$ or $N = 1$,
so $c \to 0$. This does not mean the particle has zero inertia or zero mass — its
rest mass is invariant (premise 3). Rather, it means:
- No electromagnetic signals can propagate.
- No clocks exist (atomic frequencies $\nu \propto c^2 \to 0$).
- No rulers exist (Bohr radius $\propto c^{-1} \to \infty$).
- No gravitational interaction is possible ($G \propto c^{-2} \to \infty$, but no
  other mass to interact with).

Physics becomes trivial in the sense that no observational comparison can be made.
The particle exists, but nothing happens. This is arguably a cleaner Machian statement
than Sciama's: instead of saying inertia vanishes (which is hard to test), the model
says the kinematic arena vanishes. It is the difference between "the particle cannot
move" and "there is nothing to move relative to or measure with."

## Open Questions

- A derivation of $c \propto N$ from more fundamental principles — is there a
  variational principle, an information-theoretic argument, or a holographic bound
  that enforces this relation?
- The normalization: $c \propto N$ gives the scaling but not the constant of
  proportionality. What sets $k$ in $c = kR^3$? Is it related to $G$, $\hbar$,
  or other fundamental constants?
- The fork (count vs. mass): the BBN discriminator has not been computed. A
  calculation of the light-element yields for each premise variant would provide a
  genuine test.
- The asymmetric-symmetry resolution: can the count and mass formulations be reconciled
  by a more careful definition of "particle" that excludes off-shell virtual quanta
  (which would make the two premises agree even in the radiation era)?
