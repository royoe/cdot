
# T10 — Galaxy Size Evolution

*Note: The model's prediction r_e∝(1+z)^{-1} is **withdrawn** in cdot-4. It rested on orbital expansion r∝c² (T9, cdot-3), which followed from G∝c⁻². With invariant G, orbits are static and the orbital-expansion driver is removed. The observational compactness of high-z galaxies is not claimed as model support and is explained within ΛCDM by mergers, formation conditions, and adiabatic expansion.*

## Former Prediction (Withdrawn)

### [Historical — superseded]

Retains the cdot-3 derivation (r ∝ c² → r_e ∝ (1+z)^{-1}) for historical record, then explains:

- The derivation depends on G ∝ c⁻².
- LLR (T8) bounds |Ġ/G| < 10⁻¹² yr⁻¹, ruling out G ∝ c⁻² at cosmologically significant rates.
- cdot-4 adopts invariant G; with G = const, r ∝ c² does not follow; orbits are static.
- Prediction r_e ∝ (1+z)^{-1} is therefore withdrawn.

### No mergers required — advantage no longer claimed

States explicitly that this formerly claimed advantage does not apply in cdot-4.
